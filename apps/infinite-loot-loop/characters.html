Multipliers shape the run

Character multipliers change how efficiently base stats become combat power. A strong attack multiplier rewards offensive equipment and shorter battles, while health or defense multipliers create a safer route through unfamiliar enemies. Agility and luck can support different strategies, but they should be judged alongside the character's complete profile rather than as isolated numbers.

The Warrior is a balanced reference point for learning the game. Other characters become more attractive when you understand which resource limits your progress. If a route fails because battles last too long, offense may be the answer. If a boss defeats you before your damage matters, durability deserves priority.

Owned bonuses are account-wide progress

Some characters provide an owned bonus that remains active after the character is obtained. These bonuses are displayed separately because they are not the same as the multipliers used while playing that character. Premium characters should be evaluated transparently: the roster identifies them as premium and shows the exact permanent utility they provide.

No single roster choice replaces equipment, routing, and stat allocation. Use the character page to establish a build direction, then compare items that benefit from that direction. For the clearest comparison, look at the two strongest multipliers, the weakest multiplier, unlock requirements, and the owned bonus together.
