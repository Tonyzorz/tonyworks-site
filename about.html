About the studio

Tony Works builds games and explains how they work.

Tony Works is an independent game studio operated by Tony. The current project is Infinite Loot-Loop, a mobile roguelike RPG developed in Unity for iOS and Android.

Written by Tony Works Last reviewed 18 July 2026 Contact: tonyzorz@naver.com
Studio purpose

One source for the game, its documentation, and player support

Why this site exists

Infinite Loot-Loop contains connected maps, many enemies and equipment choices, permanent collection progress, achievements, and different character multipliers. Those systems are difficult to explain inside a small mobile interface without interrupting play. Tony Works therefore maintains this site as a separate, readable companion.

The goal is not to fill pages with search keywords. The goal is to answer practical player questions: where an item comes from, what a character bonus means, how a world connects to the next one, and why a route may consume too many Adventure Points.

How the information is produced

Catalog facts are exported from the same Unity project used to build the game. Developer-written guides add context that raw numbers cannot provide. Exported facts and editorial explanations are kept visually separate so readers can tell the difference between a current game value and strategic advice.

Game development changes quickly. A future build can adjust balance, rewards, maps, or purchase availability. Pages therefore show review dates, link to patch notes, and describe the data process. When a discrepancy is reported, the game source and current store build are checked before the site is corrected.

Original work

The game, companion tools, written guides, and exported reference data are maintained by Tony Works. Third-party articles are not copied into the wiki.

Transparent monetization

Advertisements are not placed on legal, loading, error, navigation, search-only, or empty pages. Editorial content remains the focus of any monetized page.

Player contact

Questions, corrections, privacy requests, and support issues can be sent directly to tonyzorz@naver.com.

Editorial principles

Each guide should help a player make a decision. Articles identify their subject, explain the relevant game system, link to supporting catalog data, and avoid promises that cannot be verified. Balance recommendations are described as strategy rather than absolute fact. Store availability and premium features are labeled clearly.

Automated exports are checked for incomplete names, orphaned records, impossible routes, missing images, and values that no longer match the active game configuration. The detailed game-data methodology explains those checks and the limits of the catalog.
